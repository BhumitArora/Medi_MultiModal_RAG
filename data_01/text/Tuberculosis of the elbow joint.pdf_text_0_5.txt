Conclusion  The basis for finding the diagnosis is a detailed, interdisciplinary diagnostic process, especially in patients 
with persisting unspecific symptoms, since joint tuberculosis is frequently the only site of manifestation. Despite 
the slow growth of mycobacteria, the microbiological findings, particularly the resistogram, should be awaited 
since extrapulmonary tuberculosis is often multidrug resistant. As shown in this case, surgical treatment is important 
for reliable diagnosis, including pathogen identification, but it is not mandatory for successful healing and regaining 
functionality of the affected joint.
